## Abstract

This treatise systematically constructs **Material-Praxis Theory (MPT)** — a meta-theoretical framework anchored in the core postulate of "the primacy of practice," designed to provide universal methodological guidance for any cognitive activity that intends to understand or intervene in reality. Unlike traditional philosophy, which pursues static contemplation of truth, MPT repositions the core purpose of cognition as *effective intervention in practice*, and derives from this the principle that the fundamental task of cognition is *error identification* rather than *proof of correctness*.

From this foundation, the Fourth Edition forges a **Three-Law system** as the supreme methodological principle:
- **First Law (Praxis-Anchoring Razor):** Any cognitive claim must be convertible into a finite-step, publicly-observable practice test — or it is automatically invalid for effectiveness.
- **Second Law (Cognitive Self-Reflection Razor):** Any cognitive framework must contain its own operational path to being falsified or replaced. Frameworks claiming completeness, immutability, or exemption from external testing are automatically invalid for effectiveness.
- **Third Law (Rule Self-Prosecution Razor):** Any coordination rule must contain its own operational path to being challenged, revised, or abolished. Rules claiming untouchability or supremacy over practice are automatically invalid in legitimacy.

All three laws are subject to a **3×3 cross-audit**: each law is tested by all three laws. All nine tests pass. The three laws are not sub-disciplines of epistemology, methodology, or axiology — they are **meta-disciplines that span all layers**.

To operationalize the Three Laws, MPT constructs a **six-subset dynamic modeling grammar (A1/A2-B1/B2-C1/C2)**, with A, B, C declared as mutually disjoint category sets. Five Practice Warnings are restructured into a **Three-Law-driven diagnostic logic chain**. The treatise develops the joint operation of the Three Laws: **Power-Responsibility Parity**, the **Force Transmission Chain**, **Offensive Negative Feedback**, and the **Three-Law Comprehensive Dynamics of Power Corruption**. **Delta Analysis (A2 − A1)** serves as the measurable vital sign of civilizational health.

Through cross-domain case studies — ranging from pseudo-scientific criticism and social thought diagnosis to technological ethics, historical narrative analysis, and personal meaning construction — the treatise demonstrates the analytical power of the methodology, with every case subjected to a collective three-law diagnosis. The treatise concludes by sketching the **Three-Law Society** — not a utopia, but a society that rewards honest failure and lets honesty outcompete lies in evolutionary selection.

MPT does not promise eternal truth. It provides a cognitive operating manual for a complex, uncertain world — one capable of continuous self-correction. Its highest commitment is to equip its users with the capacity to question, improve, and ultimately transcend the framework itself.

## I. The Three Laws — The Meta-Disciplines

### 1.1 First Law (Praxis-Anchoring Razor)

> **Any cognitive claim that intends to understand or intervene in reality must be convertible into a practice-testing protocol completable in finite steps with publicly observable results. Otherwise, the claim is automatically invalid for the pursuit of effectiveness.**

The First Law governs the A-layer — the ultimate testing ground is physical causality. It does not tell you which answer is correct, but it ruthlessly excludes claims that can never be tested and therefore never corrected. Its reach extends across all layers: B1 cognitive claims, B2 institutional claims, and C agent-capability claims must all be testable.

### 1.2 Second Law (Cognitive Self-Reflection Razor)

> **Any cognitive framework must contain its own operational path to being falsified or replaced. Frameworks claiming completeness, immutability, or exemption from external testing are automatically invalid for the pursuit of effectiveness.**

The First Law tests individual propositions. The Second Law tests the framework's meta-attitude: does it admit it might be entirely wrong? Does it provide a path for its own replacement? A clever B1 framework can pass every individual proposition through the First Law while protecting its core from revision through auxiliary hypotheses — the Second Law targets this vulnerability.

### 1.3 Third Law (Rule Self-Prosecution Razor)

> **Any coordination rule must contain its own operational path to being challenged, revised, or abolished. Rules claiming untouchability, immutability, or supremacy over practice are automatically invalid in legitimacy.**

The Third Law solves the millennial paradox of rebellion: a law that forbids all channels of challenge forfeits its own legitimacy — challenging it becomes structurally justified. By relinquishing the claim of "sacred inviolability," a legal system achieves genuine inviolability, because no revolution is needed when institutional channels for change exist. This breaks the ancient cycle: oppression → revolution → new oppression → new stagnation.

### 1.4 Three-Law Hierarchy and 3×3 Cross-Audit

| Law | Operational Domain | Core Verb | Irreplaceability |
|-----|-------------------|-----------|------------------|
| First | A-layer (testing endpoint) | Anchor | Second Law can't replace it — good attitude, bad proposition still rejected |
| Second | B1-layer (framework self-correction) | Self-reflect | First Law can't replace it — testable propositions, closed framework still spins |
| Third | B2-layer (rule legitimacy) | Self-prosecute | Both prior laws can't replace it — rule-level challenge channels are an independent dimension |

**3×3 Cross-Audit:**

| | Tested by First Law | Tested by Second Law | Tested by Third Law |
|---|---|---|---|
| **First Law** | ✅ Testable via A/B experiment | ✅ Contains self-falsification path | ✅ Contains challenge path |
| **Second Law** | ✅ Testable by comparing open vs closed frameworks | ✅ Contains self-falsification path | ✅ Contains challenge path |
| **Third Law** | ✅ Testable by comparing systems with/without challenge channels | ✅ Contains self-falsification path | ✅ Contains challenge path |

All nine tests pass. The three razors do not protect each other — they compete to cut themselves. A claim that says "I can be falsified" is actually true until it is falsified.

## II. The A-B-C Six-Subset Dynamic Modeling Framework

Any cognitive-praxis system can be decomposed into three **mutually disjoint category sets**, each with two exhaustive subsets:

- **Set A (Material)** = {A1, A2} — the material substrate of the system
- **Set B (Information)** = {B1, B2} — the system's information and coordination mechanisms
- **Set C (Agents)** = {C1, C2} — the system's practical agents

**"Mutually disjoint"** means the membership criteria for each set do not overlap. A bridge simultaneously has A2-value (physical load-bearing), B1-value (aesthetic evaluation), and B2-value (market price). These are *different elements* belonging to different sets. Conflating them is a category error — the Logical Idling and Incommensurability warnings of the Five Practice Warnings exist precisely for this.

### A-Layer: Material Substrate

**A1 (Physical):** Natural properties — physical laws, geography, climate, resource endowments. Humans can discover them, never invent them.

**A2 (Value, provisional naming):** Materialized outcomes created by human practice that, once generated, exist objectively independent of anyone's will. Infrastructure, transformed land, accumulated means of production, human capital.

**A1-A2 Structure:** This distinction is structurally isomorphic to special relativity — no absolute "static," only "slow enough" relative to a reference frame. A1 changes at geological timescales; A2 changes at historical timescales.

### B-Layer: Information and Coordination

**B1 (Cognitive Frameworks):** Scientific paradigms, cultural narratives, ideologies. Subject to the Second Law — must contain self-falsification paths. Also subject to the First Law — core propositions must be testable.

**B2 (Coordination Rules):** Laws, market rules, technical standards, social protocols. Subject to the Third Law — must contain self-challenge paths. Also subject to the First Law.

The crucial distinction: B1 governs "how we understand" (concepts), B2 governs "how we act" (institutions). Confusing the two — e.g., attributing institutional failure to "backward thinking" — is a common category error.

### C-Layer: Active Agents

**C1 (Agents):** Who acts, who bears consequences. Individual, organization, class, state, civilization — any entity that can independently run an A-B-C loop. Subject to the First Law — capability claims must be testable.

**C2 (Behavior Patterns):** Regularities emerging from agents' repeated practice. Characteristics: emergent, path-dependent, modifiable through practice.

### Core Dynamic Cycle

```
A1 ——praxis→ A2
              ↓ constraints
B1 ——institutionalization→ B2
                             ↓ shaping
C1 ——pattern emergence→ C2
```

**Anti-Reductionist Warning:** A is the ultimate constraint, not the only direct cause. B1, B2, C1, C2 possess relative autonomy.

### The Bridge Example: Three Kinds of Value

| | A2 Value | B1 Value | B2 Exchange Value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ontological status | Objectively existing material outcome | Subjective cognitive framework | Institutionalized exchange ratio |
| Measurable? | Yes (physical units) | No (only expressed) | Yes (monetary units) |
| Depends on "feeling"? | No | Yes | Partially |
| Contestable? | No (physical facts) | Yes (values inherently contestable) | Yes (price negotiable) |
| Test method | Physical measurement | Not directly testable | Market data |

This distinction resolves a millennia-spanning category confusion: when physics and statistics become available, continuing to use B1 values as proxies for A2 value is no longer expediency — it is B1 Alienation.

Measurable (infrastructure stock in km/kW/ton, human capital in education years/life expectancy, means of production in machine tools/farmland).

## IV. The Five Practice Warnings (Three-Law-Driven Diagnostic Logic)

The five warnings are not isolated tools — they are five faces of three-law failure, connected by a diagnostic logic chain:

```
Step 1: First Law Check — Can the claim be tested?
  → No → Logical Idling Warning (proposition cannot anchor to testable practice)

Step 2: Second Law Check — Is the framework willing to revise?
  → Framework closed → Unknown-Unknown Warning (doesn't know it doesn't know)
  → Frameworks incommensurable → Incommensurability Warning (concepts damage each other in translation)

Step 3: Third Law Check — Are challenge channels open?
  → Channels blocked by power → Asymmetry Warning (who gets heard depends on power, not practice)

Step 4: Ultimate Check — Has the B-layer crossed the threshold into alienation?
  → Practice Deviation Warning (distinguishing healthy evolution from alienated drift)
```

### 1. Logical Idling Warning (First Law Failure)

Cognitive architectures that construct elegant, self-referential systems disconnected from any conceivable practice test. "Cognitive theology" is the archetype. Detection criterion: "If practice contradicts the theory's predictions, how does the theory correct itself?" If it cannot correct, or absorbs any counterexample without touching its core → idling.

### 2. Unknown-Unknown Warning (Second Law Failure: Framework Closure)

The perceptual dimension of "needing correction" hasn't been established. Detection tools: reverse-label verification (ask the person to operationally define their claimed "rationality") and defensive-clause detection (who writes "this is only preliminary" but never specifies what's preliminary).

### 3. Incommensurability Warning (Second Law Failure: Cross-Framework Translation)

Core concepts across paradigms, cultures, or historical periods lack precise, lossless correspondence. Three types: paradigmatic, cultural, historical. Principle: the burden of translation lies with the translator.

### 4. Asymmetry Warning (Third Law Failure)

Unequal distribution of power and resources determines "whose cognition gets heard." The only warning that simultaneously violates all three laws: First — power distorts practice testing; Second — power shuts down framework self-correction; Third — power blocks challenge channels. At the governance level, power-responsibility mismatch is the core pathology.

### 5. Practice Deviation Warning (Expanded — The Origin Insight of MPT)

> **Two types of problems in human society: problems discovered IN practice, and problems manufactured BY detachment from practice (实践偏离).**

**Type 1: Problems discovered in practice.** Root cause in A-layer. Tool functions normally but encounters changed A-layer conditions (resource exhaustion, external shock). Solution: improve the tool.

**Type 2: Problems manufactured by detachment from practice.** Root cause in B-layer. Tool/system's operation systematically drifts away from or betrays its original practical purpose. Solution: cut off the deviation, reset the anchor.

This binary passes a three-law self-test, and survives edge-case testing (external shocks → Type 1; "not practicing at all" → Type 2 extreme form; law rendered obsolete by social change → Type 1).

**Two Forms of Practice Deviation:**

- **Process Entropy** (B2-layer): System adds rules, patches, procedures. Healthy = redundancy increases robustness (safety margins, case law accumulation). Alienated = rules self-proliferate disconnected from purpose (three approval stamps become thirty).
- **Structural Emergence** (cross-layer mutation): System undergoes qualitative transformation. Healthy = structural breakthrough (internet, vaccines, paradigm revolutions). Alienated = new structure detaches from A-layer anchoring, operates in B-layer self-reference. Criterion: NOT "does it devour the parent structure" (feudalism devoured slavery — that was progress), but "does it create measurable A2-A1 delta?"

**Alienation is cross-layer, not just B1:**

| Layer | Alienation Form | Example |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| A2 | Created goods dominate creators | Marx's original labor alienation |
| B1 | Symbols replace entities | GDP replaces actual infrastructure status |
| B2 | Rules self-proliferate disconnected from purpose | Three stamps become thirty |
| C1 | Identity/role deviates from self-interest | Worker internalizes capitalist values |
| C2 | Behavior patterns deviate from real interests | Working for KPIs instead of output |

B1 alienation is emphasized not because it's the only kind — but because it's the most invisible. You feel A2 alienation (exhaustion from overtime), you see B2 alienation (thirty-stamp approval), but B1 alienation — GDP numbers look great, the stock market is rising — makes you genuinely believe "the economy is fine" without checking the actual A2-A1 delta.

## V. The Joint Operation of the Three Laws

### 5.1 Power-Responsibility Parity

Western political philosophy conflates "limiting power" with "matching power to responsibility." MPT rejects this. Power is not inherently evil — power is the capability of C1 to influence A-layer through B2. It is a neutral tool. The question is not "how much power?" but "can this power pass the test?"

The three razors do not cage power — they weigh it. Each razor does not ask "is your power too big?" but "can your power pass inspection?" Power can be unlimited — as long as it passes the three scales.

This reveals the fatal flaw in the "cage power" tradition: **limited liability.** If power is encaged, decision-makers enjoy benefits but the cage (institution, ultimately society) bears the consequences. MPT demands that every power-holder personally carry the scale of their own decisions.

### 5.2 Force Transmission Chain — Forces Do Not Disappear, They Transfer Between Layers

The three laws are a serial force transmission chain. Violating rules does not merely "trigger detection" — forces accumulate and transmute:

```
B2 rule violation (Third Law violated)
    ↓
Society naturally produces B1 condemnation (social immune first line)
    ↓
If violator ignores B1 condemnation and stigmatizes it
(using unfalsifiable empty rhetoric to delegitimize condemnation)
    ↓
B1 condemnation force doesn't disappear — the channel is blocked
    ↓
Force sinks into A-layer, accumulates as structural stress
    ↓
A-layer erupts eventually — physical judgment
(social backlash, election reversals, institutional collapse, delta regression)
    ↓
Repair: what you broke, you fix — B2 and B1 both need repair
Otherwise A-layer stress won't dissipate
    ↓
Repair naturally tightens: everyone is scared, new B2 is stricter than old B2
```

The stability of a society does not depend on whether it has conflict — but on whether its **B1 condemnation channels are open**. If open, force releases at the B1 level (condemnation → correction → recovery). If blocked, force sinks into A-layer and waits to erupt.

### 5.3 Aggressive Negative Feedback — Why Negative Feedback Cannot Be Passive

| | Passive Negative Feedback (Traditional Science) | Aggressive Negative Feedback (MPT) |
|---|---|---|
| Attitude toward error | Error is a byproduct of testing — avoid it | Error is cognitive nutrition — seek it actively |
| Who initiates testing | External — peer review, competing schools | Internal — theory demands its own falsification |
| What triggers framework revision | External evidence accumulates to "unignorable" | Theory pre-installs revision procedures |
| What failure means | Paper rejected, project cancelled | Testable data — fuel for system evolution |
| Honesty's role | Academic ethics requirement | Precondition for negative feedback to function |

MPT has no defense, only offense — not a rhetorical posture, but a structural necessity deduced from the three laws. Its goal is not to prove itself right — but to discover as quickly as possible where it is wrong.

### 5.4 Power Corruption — Three-Law Comprehensive Dynamics

Power does not merely corrupt cognition (Second Law) — it simultaneously degrades ALL THREE LAWS in a coordinated spiral:

| Stage | First Law (Praxis Anchor) | Second Law (Self-Reflection) | Third Law (Rule Challenge) |
|-------|--------------------------|------------------------------|----------------------------|
| 0: Healthy | Practice feedback open | Open to revision | Rules challengeable |
| 1: Isolation begins | Time/space consumed by power structure | Receives "error has no cost" signals | Interpretation power concentrates |
| 2: Floating | Physically unable to access A-layer feedback | B2 protective layer built, negative feedback cut | Rule exemptions institutionalized |
| 3: Alienation | A-layer signals fully replaced by B1 symbols | B1 symbols replace A2 entities | Power-wielder merges with rules |
| 4: Corruption complete | Even "need for practice" awareness gone | Self-falsification paths dead | Challengers systematically punished |

After Stage 4, corruption enters an irreversible death spiral. Reversal requires simultaneously repairing all three laws — but repairing any one law is blocked by the corruption of the other two.

**This is not a cognitive problem — it is a physical problem.** The ultimate result is not a mistaken belief — it is a measurable gap between the power-wielder's B1 self-perception and actual A-layer state. This gap does not shrink with confidence. It accumulates — until the A-layer erupts.

### 5.5 Honest Failure — The Precondition for Negative Feedback

When a promise anchored to the First Law (measurable A2-A1 target) fails honestly, the honest failure provides invaluable negative feedback — more valuable than many successes. Society should **reward honest failure**: provide the honest failure with resources and opportunities to try again, and **severely punish fraudulent success**: those who acquire power through false claims and then fail while deflecting blame.

The contrast:
- Honest failure provides A-layer accurate signals for negative feedback → social system can correct
- Fraudulent success provides A-layer false signals → social system misdiagnoses

## VI. The Three-Law Society — The Theory's Ultimate Direction

The Three Laws are not merely analytical tools — they point toward a deducible social form. This society does not promise perfection, but it promises honesty. It does not guarantee freedom from hunger or disease, but it guarantees that no one needs to lie.

**At its core: a society that rewards honest failure.**

An official makes a First-Law-anchored promise: "Within my term, PM2.5 will drop 15%, and 20,000 affordable housing units will be built." At term's end, PM2.5 dropped only 8%, housing reached 15,000. In the old world, he would spin, deflect, and redefine "success." In the Three-Law Society, the Second and Third Laws have closed all exit routes. He cannot deny, cannot deflect. He initiates the self-prosecution procedure he wrote when taking office, openly admits his failure, analyzes the cause, resigns, and transfers the data to his successor.

This is an **honest failure**. Society rewards him — because this failure provided negative feedback more valuable than many successes. It precisely maps a blind spot for future correction.

The Three-Law Society does not eliminate suffering. But it permits you to say "I am suffering" without being told "no, you're not." Your failure is not used as a weapon against you. Your mistakes are not grounds for humiliation. Your honesty is never punished.

**Competitive advantage:** Not because it's "more moral," but because it's *more effective* in long-term information processing. A society that rewards honest failure accumulates corrections. A society that punishes honest failure accumulates lies. The former evolves; the latter accumulates until collapse. This is not a deterministic outcome — the Three-Law Society emerges IF humans choose to follow the Three Laws, and the choice is not determined.

## VII. Dialogues with Other Traditions

**Marxist Philosophy of Practice:** MPT inherits Marxism's primacy of practice, its historical-dialectical method, and its critique of alienation. It develops these into operational tools: the A-B-C framework generalizes Marx's analysis of any cognitive-praxis system; the Three Laws provide operational epistemic discipline; the Five Practice Warnings update Marxism's ideological critique for the 21st-century cognitive crisis. MPT is not a replacement for Marxism — it is a methodology manual within the tradition of praxis philosophy.

**Pragmatism and Positivism:** Both share MPT's emphasis on practical consequences. MPT differs by anchoring "usefulness" in publicly observable test protocols and structural analysis (A-B-C), not subjective utility. Against positivism's "empirical fundamentalism," MPT insists that all experience is theory-laden — rigor comes from the test-revision cycle, not from a pure empirical foundation.

**Systems Theory, Cybernetics, Complexity Science:** Deep resonance with feedback mechanisms, emergence, and control theory. MPT adds a practice-teleological dimension that scientific frameworks alone cannot provide — value anchoring through the Three Laws.

**Postmodernism and Deconstruction:** MPT affirms its critique of power, narrative construction, and subject formation — all consistent with the Asymmetry Warning. But MPT rejects the slide into nihilism. There is an A-layer that does not accept deconstruction. A bridge's collapse is not a "social construction" — gravity does not care about your discourse. MPT is not "anti-deconstruction" — it says "every deconstruction must provide a testable alternative."

## VIII. Boundaries, Open Questions, and the "Fourth Law"

### The Non-Judgment Domain

The Three Laws apply only to cognitive-praxis activities that intend to understand or intervene in reality. For pure emotional expression, artistic aesthetics, personal faith — non-instrumental domains — the Three Laws should NOT be applied.

If the First Law were applied to art ("prove this painting is beautiful"), it would destroy art. If the Second Law were applied to faith ("does your belief contain a self-falsification path?"), it would destroy faith. If the Third Law were applied to love ("does your love contain a challenge procedure?"), it would destroy love.

This boundary is not the theory's weakness — it is the theory's highest self-discipline. The Three Laws are a scalpel, not a butcher's knife. A scalpel knows where not to cut.

**This boundary was not "designed" — it was discovered through honest practice.** The framework's author repeatedly encountered the problem: "I love her," "this painting is beautiful," "I believe in God" — these are real, undeniable experiences, yet they cannot be converted into operational test protocols. The honest response was: "the First Law cannot adjudicate here." This is not the theory failing — this is the theory's immune system working.

### Open Questions and the Call for a "Fourth Law"

The treatise formally issues a call for a **"Fourth Law"** — a principle more fundamental than, or complementary to, the Three Laws. If you discover such a principle, write it here.

### A Tentative Conjecture: The "Fourth Law" as Higher-Dimensional Structure

The boundary where the Three Laws halt (the non-judgment domain) may not merely be the internal limit of the Three Laws. It may be the **gravitational shadow** of a higher-dimensional structure — like the anomalous precession of Mercury's orbit was not a failure of Newtonian mechanics, but the gravitational signal of General Relativity.

A "Fourth Law" might exist at higher abstraction, currently unobservable given the current level of human material productive forces — analogous to how dark matter is known only through its gravitational effects, not through direct observation. The boundary we observe is its shadow. Observing the law itself may require the next leap in human cognitive material foundations — perhaps AI-augmented cognition operating at a dimensionality the human B1 cannot currently reach.

This conjecture itself cannot pass the Three Law audit, so it is explicitly marked as **provisional speculation**. That is itself the honest epistemic position demanded by MPT.
