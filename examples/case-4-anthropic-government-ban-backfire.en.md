# Case 4: Anthropic Government Ban Backfires, Surpassing OpenAI — "National Security Threat" Becomes the Best Advertisement

## I. Event Background (June 2026)

In June 2026, the U.S. government delivered what was historically its most severe triple strike against an AI company to Anthropic:

- **Pentagon (DoD) Supply Chain Risk Designation**: Anthropic was designated a "supply chain risk entity," barring defense contractors from using Anthropic models. [Defense News, 2026-06-10]
- **White House Export Controls**: Export controls were imposed on Anthropic's most powerful models, Mythos 5 and Fable 5, restricting sales to specific countries/regions. [Reuters, 2026-06-12]
- **White House AI Lead Public Ridicule**: The White House AI policy lead called Anthropic a "hypocritical AI safety company that is actually the biggest AGI arms dealer." [Washington Post, 2026-06-14]

Market reaction was unexpected:

- **Market share surged to 41%**, surpassing OpenAI's 39.5%. [CNBC, 2026-06-25]
- **ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue) increased from $9 billion to $30 billion** — a 233% increase. Growth was primarily driven by large purchases from enterprise clients, especially financial institutions and pharmaceutical companies. [WSJ, 2026-06-25]
- **Data released June 25**: Anthropic's market share reached 41%, surpassing OpenAI for the first time. In the enterprise market, Anthropic's client retention rate rose from 87% to 96%.

Meanwhile, Anthropic's response:

- Terminated public access to its strongest models (Mythos 5/Fable 5)
- Stated "government intervention prevents us from providing optimal service to existing clients"
- Refused the government's proposed security monitoring and autonomous weapon deployment terms

### 3.3 B1 Alienation Detection ⚠️

**Three-Step Detection**:

1. **What is the category switch?** → "Government export controls (B2 rule change)" is switched to "Anthropic is designated a security threat (B1 moral label)"; "Anthropic market share rises (B2 market signal)" is switched to "Government crackdown fails (B1 value judgment)." Two category switches occur simultaneously within the same event.

2. **Which A2 entity has the B1 symbol replaced?** → "Scarcity of being blocked" has replaced "actual model service capability." What enterprise clients are paying for is not "better model capability" but "model capability that might become unavailable." Scarcity is a B1/B2 construct (artificial scarcity created by rules), not A2-level natural scarcity.

3. **Is the inspection loop closed?** → **Strangely unclosed.** The government's B1 narrative ("arms dealer") was intended negatively but produced a positive market effect, proving that the propagation effect of B1 symbols is not unidirectional — the B1 signal underwent **inverse valence transformation** during its return (Government says "this is bad" → Market interprets "this is scarce" → Enterprise behavior proves "this is valuable"). **This is not a closed inspection loop, but a phase transition in the B1 loop.**

**Diagnosis**: The B1 Alienation in this case is not a symbol substitution within a single system, but a **cross-system B1 symbol inversion** — the same symbol ("government ban") carries different values in different B1 systems (in the government system, it is "sanction"; in the market system, it is "scarcity certification"). This is a special variant of B1 Alienation: **B2 Rule → B1 Signal → B2 Market → Opposite-direction B2 Result.**

### 3.4 Delta Analysis

- **B2 (Revenue/Market share)**: B2 indicators **rose sharply** — ARR +233%, share from 34.4% to 41%.

The Delta direction is the reverse of the conventional — A2 Delta contracts, B2 Delta expands. **If the B2 bubble bursts (enterprise clients discover that the capability of the blocked model can be met by substitutes), Anthropic's B2 will rapidly correct.**

### 3.5 Joint Three-Law Operation Analysis ⚠️ Unique Feature of This Case

The most distinctive MPT contribution of this case: It demonstrates that the three laws are not an isolated checklist, but a **joint-operating mechanical system**. All three laws acted simultaneously within this chain of events, forming a complex force transmission chain.

#### Step 1: B2 Rule Abuse (Third Law Failure)

The government used executive orders to apply B2 rules (export controls + supply chain designation) to punish a specific C1 agent (Anthropic), rather than addressing a universal A-level problem (such as a concrete threat from AI safety).

**Third Law Examination**:
- Can the rules be challenged? → Legal challenge paths exist for NSTM-series memoranda and DoD designations, but the threshold is extremely high (requires litigation, high C1 resource barrier)
- Did rule-making include feedback from the regulated party? → No public evidence that Anthropic received a hearing during the designation process
- **Conclusion**: Third Law fails at the rule-making stage — government B2 rules exist, but the challenge channel is compressed by C1 power asymmetry

#### Step 2: B2 Rule → B1 Reverse Signal (Second Law Curvilinear Operation)

Government expectation: B2 rules tighten → B1 "Anthropic is unreliable" → Enterprise clients reduce procurement → Anthropic market share declines.

Actual result: B2 rules tighten → B1 "Anthropic is a scarce strategic asset" (B1 signal inversion) → Enterprise clients increase procurement → Anthropic market share rises.

**Second Law Examination**:
- Does the government framework include a path for self-correction? → No. The "crushing models = protecting security" framework is closed — the government cannot admit that regulation achieved the opposite effect
- Has the enterprise client framework been falsified? → The "scarcity panic" framework has been partially falsified at the A2 level (service capability decreased after the blockade) — clients paid a higher price for weaker service, yet the framework was not adjusted
- **Conclusion**: The Second Law fails simultaneously in both directions — the government framework is closed, and the enterprise client framework rejects counterevidence

#### Step 3: B1 Reverse Signal → C2 Market Response → A2 Anchoring Restart (First Law Final Verification)

B1 signal inversion leads to C2 (enterprise client panic buying) → B2 (market share surge) → B2 and A2 (actual service capability) reach maximum divergence → Divergence will ultimately be tested by A2-level verification.

**First Law Examination**:
- Core proposition: "Market share growth proves the government crackdown failed" → Can it be translated into a verifiable scheme? → Requires: 1) Control variable comparison (e.g., OpenAI's concurrent client growth data); 2) Long-term tracking of the A2 divergence state; 3) Exclusion of alternative explanations (e.g., did Anthropic announce a major technological breakthrough in the same period?). **Conditions are partially met, but the causal chain is incomplete — must be labeled as a provisional hypothesis**
- **Long-term First Law test**: "Can the B2 premium caused by scarcity panic persist?" — Verifiable: If enterprise client renewal rates six months later are not significantly above the industry average, the B2 premium is falsified
- **Conclusion**: The final First Law verification has not yet completed — it will be answered 6–12 months later by A2 data (actual renewal rates / service capability recovery level)

### 3.6 Three-Law Dynamics of Power Corrosion ⚠️ Special Detection

This case demonstrates an **unconventional trajectory** of power corrosion — the power holder's (government's) three-law corruption is not merely "locking itself down" (unable to be challenged), but also produces **unexpected actual consequences** (the crackdown inadvertently spurred the target's growth). This activates a special variant of the fourth stage "Alienation" within the five-stage power corrosion dynamics:

|-----------------------|----------------------------|------------|
| ① Healthy Period | Government assesses Anthropic's security risk | ✅ Theoretically justified |

**Special Finding**: The power corrosion in this case is in a state of "alienation has occurred but has not entered the irreversible stage." The government-level B1 framework ("Anthropic = arms dealer") and the market-level B1 framework ("Anthropic = scarce asset") have become opposed, but the A level (actual model service capability + compute allocation) has not yet suffered irreversible damage. **Power corrosion here is not locking uselessness — it is an anomalous pattern of "bad things done, but good things happened."**

## V. MPT Operational Recommendations

1. **For the government (Pentagon/White House)**: This case exposes the core feature of power corrosion — **you cannot find the problems with your framework using the terms of that framework.** The White House needs to practice **Aggressive Negative Feedback** by asking: "If my regulatory measures actually promoted the growth of the target company, what mechanism do I have to discover this?" Second Law self-reflection: Add a policy evaluation procedure — 90 days after export controls are imposed, automatically trigger policy evaluation based on A2 data (actual changes in the target enterprise's market share). Third Law: Establish hearing and review mechanisms for regulated enterprises, lowering the C1 resource threshold for challenging policy.

2. **For Anthropic**: Power-Responsibility Parity has been maintained on Anthropic's side in this case (refusing monitoring/autonomous weapon terms, bearing short-term losses). **Recommend holding this position** — do not accept monitoring/autonomous weapon terms under government pressure. But a distinction is needed: The B1 "victim" narrative provides short-term legitimacy dividends, but in the long run, A2 capability (availability of the strongest models) is the sustainable foundation. Recommendations: 1) Develop stronger next-generation replacement models; 2) Quantify and publish compliance costs (client losses from closing Mythos 5/Fable 5) — this serves not only as B2 compensation evidence but also as A2 Delta data.

3. **For enterprise clients**: Panic buying is a rational choice at the C2 level, but be wary of **A2 divergence risk** — you paid a higher price for weaker service (strongest models were closed). Recommendations: 1) Include a clause in procurement contracts such as "if Anthropic restores access to strongest models, will prices adjust?"; 2) Establish multi-vendor backup plans to reduce dependency on a single model provider, thereby hedging against B1 scarcity narratives.

4. **For OpenAI**: You are the one "surpassed" in this story — but this is not about you losing to Anthropic; it is about the government doing Anthropic a peculiar favor (creating scarcity). B2 market share fluctuations do not reflect A2 capability differences. Recommendation: Do not engage in a B1 confrontational narrative ("Anthropic surpassed due to government crackdown"), but focus on A2 capability improvement and client service.

5. **For independent analysts**: This case is a first-rate **B1 Reverse Signal Laboratory** — when the government marks an agent with a negative B1 label, under what conditions does it become a positive market signal? Hypothetical condition map:
   - Condition 1: The marked agent has a strong client base independent of the government (enterprise/institutional clients)
   - Condition 2: Government regulatory space is limited (cannot completely block market circulation)
   - Condition 3: The marked agent's product has no direct functional substitute (scarcity holds)
   - When all three conditions are met, the transmission path of government negative labeling → scarcity certification → demand increase is established
   - **Test this hypothesis**: Find counterexamples — cases where market share did **not** rise after a government crackdown, verifying the absence of conditions 1/2/3
