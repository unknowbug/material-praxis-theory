# Case Study 1: The AI Compute Narrative — A Real-Time B1 Alienation Diagnosis

## 1. Event Background (June 2026)

The technology and financial markets in June 2026 present a set of seemingly contradictory signals:

- **Cerebras Q1 Earnings**: Revenue +94% YoY to $193.4M, net loss narrowed 41%. However, the company warned that core gross margin would shrink in Q2; the stock plunged 10%+ on the day. [CNBC, 2026-06-24]
- **NVIDIA Price-Cut Rumor**: Markets circulated rumors that NVIDIA demanded PCB manufacturers cut prices by 10%, though manufacturers and market participants described the rumor as "significantly exaggerated." [Sina Finance, 2026-06-24]
- **AI Sector Selloff**: South Korean market plunged 10% in a single day; Wall Street AI concept stocks pulled back sharply. [CNN, 2026-06-24]
- **OpenAI Unveils Custom Chip "Jalapeño"**: Co-designed with Broadcom, purpose-built for LLM inference, from design to tape-out in 9 months. [Techmeme, 2026-06-24]
- **Meanwhile**: Sina Finance headlines declare "faith in AI compute scarcity begins to waver."

Behind these signals, a cognitive confusion about "what AI compute is actually worth" is unfolding.

---

## 2. Conventional AI Analysis (Without MPT)

Here is the typical analytical pattern of current mainstream AI (ChatGPT, Claude, etc., operating without MPT guidance) on this type of event:

> *"The AI compute market is undergoing a valuation recalibration. Cerebras's margin warning suggests the AI chip industry may face price-war pressure. NVIDIA's price-cut rumor, though denied, reflects market concerns about compute oversupply. OpenAI's custom chip signals vertical integration — leading AI companies are reducing dependence on NVIDIA. Overall, AI investment is transitioning from a 'scarcity premium' phase to an 'efficiency competition' phase, reminiscent of post-dot-com industry consolidation."*

**What's wrong with this analysis?** Let us deconstruct it layer by layer using MPT.

## 3. MPT v5 Layer-by-Layer Analysis

### 3.1 Four Filters of Pre-Screening

| Filter | Check | Result |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Definitional Clarity | Is "compute scarcity" clearly defined? | ⚠️ Revise — "scarcity" means GPU supply volume? Compute price? Chip performance? Three meanings conflated interchangeably |
| Burden of Proof | "Faith in compute scarcity wavering" — who holds this faith? What is the evidence? | ⚠️ Revise — no operational definition of "believers" is given; it is a B1 narrative self-reference |
| Category Error | "Stock dropped" = "compute is no longer scarce"? | ❌ Reject — B2 price fluctuation is smuggled into A2 material attribute |
| Self-Reference | Is the analysis itself using "narrative" to explain "narrative"? | ❌ Reject — "faith wavering" is itself a B1 narrative; deploying it to explain market movement is circular reasoning |

### 3.2 A-B-C Deep Parse

| Layer | Identification | Current State |
|-------|---------------|---------------|
| **A1 (Physical)** | Transistor count, process node, compute capacity (TFLOPS), power consumption (W), wafer capacity | This is the *actual* "compute." TSMC 3nm capacity, HBM memory bandwidth, lithography machine count — all physical quantities. **None of these physical quantities changed at headline-magnitude levels within a quarter.** |
| **A2 (Value)** | Actual output of AI chips: trained models, inference tokens served, deployed compute scale | Continuously growing — more models being trained, more users using inference services. A2 is **expanding.** |
| **B1 (Cognitive Framework)** | "AI compute scarcity," "AI bubble," "dot-com 2.0" — these are cognitive frameworks, not facts | **B1 is currently undergoing violent oscillation**: the same A2 growth fact triggers B1 to swing between "scarcity premium" and "oversupply collapse" |
| **B2 (Coordination Rules)** | Stock price, chip quotes, gross margin | Cerebras margin warning → stock crash. This is **B2-layer fluctuation**, not A-layer change |
| **C1 (Agent)** | Investors (institutional/retail), AI companies (OpenAI/Anthropic), chip makers (NVIDIA/Cerebras/AMD) | Investors selling, AI companies still buying — **C1 behavior diverging** |
| **C2 (Behavior Pattern)** | Panic selling, FOMO chasing, "this time is different" narratives | Classic B1→B2→C2 feedback loop: B1 "faith wavering" → B2 price drop → C2 panic selling → B2 further decline → B1 "confirmed bubble" |

### 3.3 B1 Alienation Detection ⚠️ Core Diagnosis

**Three-Step Detection**:

1. **Category Substitution**: Confirmed. The object of discussion has been substituted from "A1 physical compute capacity" to "B2 stock price of chip companies."
2. **B1 Symbols Replace A2 Entities**: Confirmed. In the headline "faith in compute scarcity wavers," the word "compute" functions as a B1 cognitive-label, not as a reference to A1/A2. No one is measuring TSMC wafer output.
3. **Closed Testing Loop**: Confirmed. "Stock dropped → proves bubble → investors sell → stock drops further → proves bubble more" — this is a fully closed B1→B2→B1 loop that never touches the A-layer.

**Diagnostic Conclusion**: Current public discussion about "AI compute" is in a state of **B1 Alienation**. B1 symbolic products (stock prices, market caps, valuation multiples) have replaced A2 entities (actual compute output and deployment volumes) as the entire basis for decision-making.

### 3.4 Delta Analysis

- **A2 (materialized net output of AI compute)**: Continuously growing — more models, more inference, more deployment. Delta state: **Positive (accumulation).**
- **B2 (market cap of AI chip companies)**: Short-term violent fluctuation — Cerebras -10%, South Korea -10%. This is a B2-layer price signal, not a delta signal.
- **Key Conclusion**: **The A2−A1 delta is expanding while B2 market caps are contracting. The two move in opposite directions — this is a classic B2 mispricing driven by B1 Alienation.**

### 3.5 Four-Quadrant Positioning

Conventional AI analysis places the discussion in **Quadrant III (Void-Referencing, Open Divergence)** — using B1 concepts ("faith," "bubble," "narrative") to explain B2 price movements, with zero A-layer anchoring throughout.

MPT analysis pulls the discussion back to **Quadrant II → Quadrant I**: first anchor in A1/A2 physical facts (chip capacity, compute deployment volumes), then ask how much of the B2 price movement is noise driven by B1 narratives and how much reflects a real slowdown in A2 growth — **this is a testable question.**

## 5. MPT Operational Recommendations

1. **For Investors**: Distinguish "Am I trading B2 volatility or evaluating A2 growth?" If the former, it's speculation, not investment.
2. **For Media**: When reporting stock prices, do not substitute "compute" for "stock price." The headline "AI faith wavers" should read "AI chip stocks drop 10%."
3. **For Analysts**: "Compute oversupply" is an A1 proposition — it requires physical data on wafer capacity, HBM bandwidth, and power supply to test. Until such data is available, any "oversupply thesis" is a B1 conjecture and must be labeled as a "tentative conjecture."
